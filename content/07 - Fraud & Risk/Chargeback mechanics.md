# Chargeback mechanics

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The "consumer protection" mechanism of the card networks that allows cardholders to dispute a transaction and have the funds forcibly returned from the merchant.

## Key Concepts
- **Reason Codes**: The specific categories used to dispute a charge (e.g., "Services not received," "Fraudulent").
- **Chargeback Lifecycle**: The sequence of events from customer dispute → issuer filing → acquirer response → final arbitration.
- **Friendly Fraud**: When a legitimate customer disputes a charge they actually made, often to avoid paying for a subscription or service.
- **Merchant Penalties**: How high chargeback rates (typically >1%) can lead to a merchant being fined or kicked off the network.
