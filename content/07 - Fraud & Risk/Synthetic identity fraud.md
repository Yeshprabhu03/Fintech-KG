# Synthetic identity fraud

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
A type of fraud where a perpetrator combines real (often stolen) and fake information to create a new, "synthetic" identity, which is then used to open fraudulent accounts and build a credit history.

## Key Concepts
- **PII Fusion**: Mixing a real SSN (often from a minor or deceased person) with a fake name and address.
- **Credit Bust-out**: The long game of building a good credit score with a synthetic identity, only to max out all credit lines and disappear.
- **Detection Difficulty**: Why synthetic identities are harder to detect than traditional stolen identities — there is no "real" victim to report the account opening.
- **CBSV (Consent Based Social Security Number Verification)**: The SSA's service that allows banks to verify if a name and SSN actually match in their records.
