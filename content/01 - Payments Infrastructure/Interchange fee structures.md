# Interchange fee structures

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The transaction fee that a merchant's bank (the acquirer) pays to the customer's bank (the issuer) during a card transaction. This note analyzes the complexity and impact of these fees.

## Key Concepts
- **Interchange Categories**: How fees vary by card type (Debit vs Credit), merchant category (MCC), and entry method (CNP vs CP).
- **Issuing Monetization**: Why interchange is the primary revenue driver for neobanks and credit card companies.
- **Regulation II (Durbin Amendment)**: The impact of US caps on debit interchange for banks with >$10B in assets.
- **Schemes & Brands**: The role of Visa and Mastercard in setting (but not collecting) these fees.
