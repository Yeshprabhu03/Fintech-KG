# Credit Scoring Models

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
Credit scoring models are the mathematical frameworks used to assess the creditworthiness of a person or business. This note explores the transition from traditional bureau-based models to modern algorithmic alternatives.

## Key Concepts
- **FICO & VantageScore**: The industry standards for credit scoring in the US.
- **Alternative Scoring**: Using non-traditional data (utility bills, rent, cash flow) to score individuals.
- **Thin-file Problem**: The challenge of scoring individuals with little to no credit history.
- **ML-based Alternatives**: Using machine learning to identify risk patterns that traditional models might miss.
