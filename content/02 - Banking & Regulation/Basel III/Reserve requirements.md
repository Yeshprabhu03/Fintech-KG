# Reserve requirements

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The amount of funds that a depository institution must hold in reserve against specified deposit liabilities, typically held as cash in the vault or at a Federal Reserve Bank.

## Key Concepts
- **Fractional Reserve Banking**: The practice where banks keep only a fraction of their deposits as reserves and lend out the rest.
- **Reserve Ratio**: The specific percentage mandated by the central bank (Note: In 2020, the Fed reduced the reserve requirement ratio to 0%).
- **Liquidity Management**: How banks manage their overnight funding via the Fed Funds Market to meet these requirements.
- **Macroeconomic Impact**: Using reserve requirements as a tool of monetary policy to control the money supply.
