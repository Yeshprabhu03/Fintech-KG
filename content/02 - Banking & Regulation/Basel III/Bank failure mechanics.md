# Bank failure mechanics

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The systematic process that occurs when a financial institution is closed by its regulator (state or federal) due to insolvency or inability to meet its obligations.

## Key Concepts
- **Receivership**: The role of the FDIC as receiver, taking control of the failed bank's assets and liabilities.
- **P&A Transaction (Purchase & Assumption)**: The most common failure resolution where a healthy bank buys the failed bank's deposits and assets.
- **The "Friday Night" Closure**: The traditional timing for bank closures to allow for weekend book reconciliation and a Monday morning reopening under a new name.
- **Deposit Payout vs. Bridge Bank**: What happens if no buyer is found immediately.
