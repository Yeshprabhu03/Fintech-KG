# OCC charter types

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The different legal structures provided by the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) that allow an institution to operate as a national bank.

## Key Concepts
- **Full-Service Charter**: The standard charter for national banks allowing for both deposit-taking and lending.
- **Fintech Charter (Special Purpose)**: The OCC's attempt to create a charter for fintechs that don't take deposits (e.g. Varo's long path to a full charter).
- **Trust Charter**: A specialized charter for institutions performing fiduciary or trust activities.
- **Preemption**: The significant legal advantage of a national charter, allowing banks to follow federal law instead of a patchwork of 50 state laws.
