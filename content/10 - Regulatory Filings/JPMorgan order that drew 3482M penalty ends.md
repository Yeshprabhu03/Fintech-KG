## Summary
The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) has terminated a March 2024 enforcement action against JPMorgan related to inadequate monitoring of trading activity across 30 global venues. The original enforcement action resulted in a $348.2M penalty. However, a companion order by the Federal Reserve appears to still be in effect.

## Key Concepts
- Enforcement action
- Trading activity monitoring
- Regulatory compliance
- OCC (Office of the Comptroller of the Currency)
- Federal Reserve

## Fintech Implications
This event highlights the importance of robust monitoring systems for financial institutions, even very large ones. Fintech companies, especially those involved in trading or market activities, should invest in comprehensive compliance solutions and risk management frameworks to avoid similar regulatory scrutiny and potential penalties. The differing actions from the OCC and Federal Reserve also demonstrate the need to understand all layers of regulatory oversight.
