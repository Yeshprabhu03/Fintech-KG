# Custody and safekeeping

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The role of financial institutions (custodian banks) in holding, protecting, and managing the assets of institutional investors, such as mutual funds or pension funds.

## Key Concepts
- **Asset Segregation**: The legal requirement to keep client assets separate from the bank's own balance sheet.
- **Corporate Actions**: The custodian's role in collecting dividends, processing stock splits, and notifying clients of proxy votes.
- **Settlement & Proxy Voting**: Finalizing trades on behalf of the client and managing their voting rights for held securities.
- **Global Custodians**: The "Big Three" (BNY Mellon, State Street, J.P. Morgan) that dominate the global safekeeping market.
