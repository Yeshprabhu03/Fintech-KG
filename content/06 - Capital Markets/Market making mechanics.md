# Market making mechanics

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The process where a firm (the market maker) provides liquidity to the market by simultaneously quoting both a buy (bid) and a sell (ask) price in a financial instrument.

## Key Concepts
- **Bid-Ask Spread**: The primary way market makers monetize their service—capturing the difference between the buying and selling prices.
- **Order Book Management**: How market makers use algorithms to maintain a balanced book and minimize exposure to directional price moves.
- **Inventory Risk**: The risk that a market maker is forced to hold a large position in a declining asset.
- **Payment for Order Flow (PFOF)**: The controversial practice where market makers pay retail brokers (like Robinhood) to route their customers' trades to them.
