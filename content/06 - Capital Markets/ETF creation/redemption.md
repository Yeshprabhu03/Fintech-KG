# ETF creation/redemption

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The "primary market" mechanism that ensures the supply of Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) shares remains in line with market demand and the price matches the fund's Net Asset Value (NAV).

## Key Concepts
- **Authorized Participants (APs)**: The large financial institutions (e.g., Goldman Sachs, Susquehanna) that have the legal right to create or redeem ETF shares.
- **Creation Unit**: A large block of ETF shares created by an AP in exchange for a "basket" of the fund's underlying securities.
- **Redemption Basket**: The process where an AP returns ETF shares to the issuer in exchange for the underlying securities.
- **Arbitrage Mechanism**: How APs monetize the difference between the ETF price and the NAV, keeping the ETF price stable.
