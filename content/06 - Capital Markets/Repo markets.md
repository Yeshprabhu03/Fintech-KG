# Repo markets

> ⚠️ Draft — needs verification against primary sources

## Summary
The repurchase agreement (repo) market is the plumbing of the short-term funding market, where institutions borrow and lend cash against collateral (typically US Treasuries).

## Key Concepts
- **Repurchase Agreement**: A sale of securities combined with an agreement to buy them back at a higher price (the repo rate) the next day or in the near future.
- **Collateral Haircut**: The difference between the market value of the collateral and the loan amount, acting as a buffer against price volatility.
- **Tri-party Repo vs. Bilateral**: The role of clearing banks (like BNY Mellon) in managing the collateral and cash exchange for the market.
- **Reverse Repo**: The perspective of the lender (the one providing the cash and receiving the collateral).
